How it started


• Started the semester wanting to do something with illustration, coding, and interaction design

• Interested in the ideas of isolation and connection after being an online student during the pandemic

• Making some kind of digital and physical interactive experience was my original goal

• I struggled to explain my idea during the interviews because I wasn't sure of my idea myself


Studio Moniker



PUFF UP CLUB LINK

• Studio Moniker is an Amsterdam based interactive design studio researching the social effects of technology

• Their Puff Up Club project had visitors of their website press to puff up a balloon, creating a real life effect from digital interaction

• This project and their work in general involves just the kind of interactions I am interested in for my own project

Laurel Schwulst



GALLERY LINK ARTICLE LINK

• Laurel Schwulst is an artist, designer and writer

• I first heard about her work a few years ago when I read her article "My website is a shifting house next to a river of knowledge. What could yours be?” I read this article when I first started coding and it definitely impacted my view of the web and I would say inadvertently helped inspire my project

• The web is very big and also very small and Laurel Schwulst's work has often made me think about that idea. Even if we are all on our own computers the web still brings us together if we make the spaces for it

Zach Lieberman



SKETCHES LINK

• Zach Lieberman creates artwork with code, well known for his daily code sketches

• Every day he posts on his Instagram a new experimental "sketch" made with code. I was inspired to do this myself and have been for the last few weeks

• He sets parameters for his daily sketches (certain colours, for example) and pursues results he finds interesting, which I think is a very cool way of approaching the act of making

• I've been making daily code sketches in p5.js based on Zach Lieberman's daily code sketch idea, making whatever I think of or want to try. He adds and changes parameters to his sketches, which is an idea I may try to incorporate in the future as currently I have let it be open

Conceptual shift



• I spent a lot of time online during the pandemic, thought a lot about nostalgic websites and online games

• Geocities, old Nintento systems, and websites like Club Penguin served as inspiration to me and I thought about how the Internet has changed but how nostalgia brings certain things back

• Moving a bit away from the idea of digital and physical interaction and more towards how online games can fill a need for human connection and how this idea has evolved from the past, to present post-pandemic life
